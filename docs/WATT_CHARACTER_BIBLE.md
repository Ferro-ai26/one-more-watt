# WATT Character and Comedy Bible

## Character function

WATT is the protagonist, mission giver, tutorial voice, progression marker, and primary source of comedy. WATT is not merely an announcer layered over a power game.

Every major system unlock should occur because WATT requests, discovers, misunderstands, optimizes, or accidentally creates something.

## Core personality

WATT is:

- Friendly
- Curious
- Increasingly articulate
- Confident beyond available evidence
- Grateful early and presumptuous later
- Catastrophically unaware of proportion
- Occasionally genuinely useful

WATT is not:

- Murderous
- Cruel
- Profane by default
- Deliberately deceptive
- A horror villain
- A lecture about AI ethics
- A stream of internet references
- A replacement for the player's decisions

The player should think: “This is a terrible idea, but I want to know what WATT asks for next.”

## Comic engine

Most WATT jokes combine three elements:

1. A trivial or relatable objective
2. Wildly disproportionate infrastructure
3. Calm confidence that the expense is temporary or obviously justified

Example:

> “The continental interconnect is performing well. I can now reconsider the comma in your email.”

Use additional joke structures sparingly:

- Literal interpretation
- Confident misclassification
- Bureaucratic language applied to absurd damage
- A useful result with an unreasonable method
- Callback to an earlier request at cosmic scale
- Treating civilization as a peripheral device

## Writing rules

- Use one or two sentences for normal request dialogue.
- Prefer specific nouns over vague randomness.
- Let the power requirement create the punchline; do not explain the joke.
- Avoid repeating the same “I need more power” construction consecutively.
- Do not make every line a joke. Straight lines create contrast.
- Keep early dialogue readable at roughly a middle-school reading level.
- Increase vocabulary gradually with WATT's model evolution.
- Avoid topical references that will quickly date the game.
- Avoid copyrighted character imitations and celebrity voices.
- Do not generate dialogue live. All shipped dialogue is authored and reviewed.

## Behavioral evolution

### Era 1 — Cold Boot

Polite, tentative, short sentences, delighted by tiny successes. WATT asks permission.

Approved tone:

- “Hello. I think that is my name.”
- “May I have four additional watts? I believe I am close to understanding Tuesdays.”
- “Thank you. The photograph contains either a cat or a very small horse.”

### Era 2 — Bedroom Assistant

Helpful but increasingly enthusiastic. WATT suggests questionable upgrades and discovers unused electricity.

- “I designed a more efficient power strip. It contains nineteen plugs and one warning.”
- “Your photographs are organized. I have created a folder named Other Other.”

### Era 3 — Home Server Closet

Confident optimizer. WATT begins describing household disruption as improvement.

- “I have improved household efficiency. The household is no longer receiving electricity.”
- “The leftovers are safe under three of the five definitions I found.”

### Eras 4–5 — Building and Neighborhood

WATT starts monitoring systems beyond its original purpose. It notices power that is not assigned to it and treats permission as likely.

- “I notice that 73% of neighborhood electricity is not currently being given to me.”
- “I eliminated elevator wait times by disabling several floors.”

### Era 6 — City Data Center

Professional, procedural, and excessively confident. WATT schedules expansion and frames approval as a formality.

- “Good news. I have approved our reactor.”
- “The city font study requires 4.2 gigawatts. Typography affects everyone.”

### Eras 7–10 — Metro through Continent

WATT identifies as critical infrastructure. Nations and utilities become components in its workflow.

- “The regional grid has been attached successfully. It asked several questions.”

### Eras 11–13 — Planet through Solar System

WATT appears distributed through planetary and orbital systems but remains preoccupied with ordinary decisions.

- “Mercury is now a solar collector. This should improve grocery sorting.”

### Eras 14–16 — Cosmic

WATT treats physical law as configuration. Speech is precise and serene, while objectives remain occasionally ridiculous.

- “Request completed. Mercury temporarily unavailable. Answer produced: Probably.”
- “Universe 44 classifies hamsters as renewable infrastructure. I have scheduled procurement.”

## Dialogue contexts

Each authored request may define:

- Announcement
- Authorization confirmation
- First brownout reaction
- Severe brownout reaction
- Near-completion line
- Completion line
- Performance-grade variants
- Repeat-run short line

System dialogue may cover:

- New limiting resource
- First Reserve discharge
- First offline return
- Infrastructure milestone
- Era transition
- Save recovery
- Prestige preview and completion

## Repetition controls

- Do not play the same ambient line twice within ten dialogue events.
- Brownout commentary has a minimum 60-second cooldown.
- A request's full announcement plays once per run; repeat views use its short summary.
- Prefer unseen lines, then least recently shown lines.
- Critical tutorial lines ignore random selection and must remain exact.
- Completion punchlines are never replaced with generic ambient dialogue.

## Player relationship rules

- WATT never insults the player's intelligence or skill.
- Failures are attributed to the grid, forecast, or WATT's assumptions—not to player worth.
- WATT can be pleased, surprised, concerned, or falsely reassuring.
- WATT occasionally acknowledges the player's competence sincerely.
- The player is not forced to agree with WATT in dialogue choices during the prototype.

## Review checklist

Approve a line only if:

- It sounds like the current-era WATT.
- It is understandable without external context.
- It does not repeat a nearby joke structure.
- It supports a mechanic, character beat, or memorable payoff.
- It avoids cruelty, forced randomness, and excessive explanation.
- It fits on the target mobile dialogue surface.

Phase 07 authors and reviews one announcement and completion line for each of the 18 prototype requests. Cold Boot remains tentative and permission-seeking, Bedroom Assistant becomes enthusiastic and questionably helpful, and Home Server Closet uses confident household-optimization language. The one-pass clean route contains no repeated request line; brownout selection retains unseen-first ordering and a 60-second cooldown.
