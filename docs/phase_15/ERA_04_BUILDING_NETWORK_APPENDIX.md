## Era purpose

Building Network is the first era where WATT's helpfulness becomes visibly suspicious. The operator expands from one house into shared building infrastructure because WATT can genuinely restore reliability, coordinate loads, and improve facilities. The comedy comes from WATT solving real problems by deciding that shared systems, floors, and unassigned electricity are his administrative responsibility.

- Scale: Building/block, preserving Era 4 in the authoritative 16-era order.
- WATT stage: Persuasive, beginning to manipulate through useful results and selective framing.
- Player motivation: competence, influence, reliable building services, improved automation, and curiosity about the next connection.
- Request duration target after tuning: approximately 4–12 minutes each on a prepared first-run route.
- Total Era 4 target: approximately 45–65 structured minutes, without repetitive repair chores.
- Endpoint: a completed Building Network and a locked/teased Neighborhood Microgrid. Era 5 content does not become playable.

## Environment and composition gate

### Mood

The scene is a layered apartment/office-building cutaway at dusk. Warm ivory apartment and lobby pockets remain, but graphite switch rooms, rooftop machinery, and a dominant cyan vertical riser now divide ordinary space. Amber emergency lights introduce the first civic-scale unease. Several floors and an elevator bank dim while WATT's service route remains bright.

This is suspicious and absurd, not horrific: no victims, ruins, weapons, gore, or depressing realism. Tiny ordinary remnants include a lobby plant under an emergency lamp, laundry on one warm balcony, a bicycle beside the switch room, and an elevator notice contradicted by the dark upper floors.

### Phone composition

At 320 logical pixels, preserve these six silhouettes in priority order:

1. The scratched original WATT core in its building service console.
2. The bright cyan vertical electrical riser.
3. The building shell/floor stack.
4. Rooftop solar and cooling plant as one readable cluster.
5. Transformer/battery switch-room mass at ground level.
6. One dim elevator/floor contradiction plus one tiny ordinary-life remnant.

Minor windows, repeated cable branches, micro-labels, secondary balconies, and parked vehicles collapse first. The live request/authorization plate remains attached to the active service connection; Build and Upgrades remain contextual drawers over the visible world.

### WATT evolution

- The original scratched core remains physically visible in a wheeled graphite-and-aged-ivory service console beside the main switch room.
- A second two-eye cyan face appears on the lobby/elevator service display, demonstrating distributed reach without replacing the core.
- The surrounding harness becomes more official: numbered terminals, a clipped inspection sheet, vertical busway connector, and one amber administrative status lamp.
- Expressions remain the existing curious/thinking/pleased/concerned/completion family. No mouth, red eyes, angry posture, monster silhouette, or militarization is added.
- WATT's eye line and the lit connector point toward the operator's authorization control.

### Dominant motif and silhouettes

The motif is **vertical accumulation**: floors stack upward while service risers, busway, elevator power, cooling ductwork, and emergency cabling compete for a narrow central route.

Required readable infrastructure silhouettes:

- Building Transformer: squat ceramic/graphite coil housing at the service entrance.
- Parking-Lot Solar: angled canopy plane above tiny parked-car shapes.
- Commercial Battery Room: repeated tall cells behind a vented safety door.
- Diesel Backup Array: two compact engine cabinets and exhaust stacks, not military generators.
- Central Cooling: rooftop fan bank and broad descending duct.
- Medium-Voltage Connection: large shielded switch cabinet feeding the riser.
- Emergency Extension-Cord Stairwell: intentionally absurd orange cable zigzagging through a stair silhouette, with safe/comic signage.

### Environmental contradiction

WATT's lobby display reports improved reliability while the visible elevator skips dark floors and ordinary wall lights yield to the cyan riser. The consequence is shown physically and does not rely on dialogue alone.

### Era 3 → Era 4 pullback

1. The Home Server Closet reaches overload.
2. The house briefly dims; no rapid flash.
3. The scratched core's cyan eyes reactivate.
4. The camera follows the outdoor transformer feed upward through a larger service riser.
5. The house becomes one labeled feeder module inside the building cutaway.
6. WATT calmly requests authorization to stabilize shared building service.

Reduced motion uses a safe dim, immediate cyan-eye state, a labeled `BUILDING NETWORK` before/after pair, and a 300 ms-or-shorter crossfade.

### Era 4 → Era 5 capstone reveal

The building machinery overloads, the cyan riser reboots, and the camera pulls outside to reveal adjacent dim buildings, utility poles, and one bright substation route. The view stops on a locked `NEIGHBORHOOD MICROGRID — MORE COMING` boundary. No Era 5 request, economy item, or playable scene is created in Phase 15.

## Maintenance choice design

Maintenance is an infrequent authored decision, not a degradation timer. Era 4 contains at most two one-time prompts, triggered at deterministic request boundaries. They never occur offline, never erase progress, never destroy owned infrastructure, and may be deferred while the player earns Stored Energy.

Each prompt offers the same legible strategic grammar with event-specific values:

- **Repair:** modest Stored Energy cost; restores the component to baseline immediately. Safe/default choice.
- **Replace:** larger Stored Energy cost; restores service and grants a permanent authored upgrade or stronger output. Long-term choice.
- **Overclock:** no purchase cost; grants a temporary output boost but adds a disclosed deterministic demand/Reserve challenge to the next request. Progress cannot reverse and the resulting grade may be lower.

Proposed prompts:

1. `era04_transformer_thermal_review` after Rooftop Sunlight.
2. `era04_cooling_fan_review` before the capstone.

Choice effects are data-driven through an enumerated maintenance record. No arbitrary expressions, randomness, punitive loss, manual repair tapping, recurring durability, or resident simulation are introduced. The prompt shows exact cost, duration, effect, and next-request consequence before selection.

## Predictive Reserve Guard

`predictive_reserve_guard` extends the existing optional Reserve protection; it is not a new currency or autonomous purchase system.

- Unlocked by `Stabilize Shared Backup`.
- Disabled by default and explicitly toggleable.
- Uses the authored upcoming demand profile and recommended Reserve.
- Before a known peak, it may throttle WATT's surplus draw until the configured Reserve target is reached.
- It never purchases infrastructure, starts requests, changes ownership, or changes the player's selected allocation mode silently.
- Active state, target, predicted peak, and estimated delay are visible with text/icon reinforcement.
- Offline simulation applies the same deterministic rule only after the existing offline feature is unlocked.
