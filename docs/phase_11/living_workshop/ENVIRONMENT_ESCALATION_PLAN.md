# Living Animated Workshop Diorama — Environment Escalation

Status: Superseded exploration under `DEC-026`; not an active environment plan.

## Scale grammar

- Eras 1–3 use recognizable physical objects and room/house cutaways.
- Eras 4–6 consolidate repeated purchases into facilities and equipment clusters.
- Eras 7–10 use representative infrastructure nodes and districts.
- Eras 11–13 use planetary/orbital systems with visible service frames.
- Eras 14–16 use megastructures and contained cosmic systems, never free-floating neon abstraction.
- Every era retains one earlier physical relic, the same WATT eye geometry, bounded cyan energy, graphite structure, amber work light, and labeled service access.

## Milestone representation

Each infrastructure family has four authored visual states:

1. `single`: first purchase appears at a named anchor.
2. `cluster`: a small readable grouping replaces individual accumulation.
3. `bank`: a consolidated cabinet/rack/array communicates scale.
4. `facility`: the family becomes an environment-scale installation or representative node.

Counts and output remain numerical. The scene never draws every owned unit.

## Depth and density

- Four reusable depth bands: shell, furniture/facility, equipment/cables, WATT/interactive foreground.
- At 320 logical pixels, no more than six high-contrast silhouettes and three simultaneously active motion cues.
- Minor labels collapse before physical silhouettes. WATT, request name, progress, bottleneck, and primary action never collapse.
- Parallax amplitude never exceeds 4 dp in routine play and can be fully disabled.
