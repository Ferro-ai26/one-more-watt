# Phase 13 — Metro to Continent

## Scope

Eras 7–10: Metropolitan Grid, Regional Utility, National Compute Grid, and Continental Supergrid.

## Objective

Expand the proven idle-management loop into multiple regions and increasingly long requests without adding repetitive micromanagement.

## Required preproduction

Before implementation, author a content/balance appendix for each era containing its infrastructure, four to eight major requests, automation unlocks, environment transition, WATT character beats, duration targets, and prestige effects. These appendices require user approval.

## Intended systems

- Several request slots or campuses
- Regional grid connections and shared Reserve
- Higher-level automation policies
- Weather/daylight/time-zone modifiers that are predictable and forecastable
- Additional Model Weight branches
- Meaningful use of early infrastructure through category synergies

## Guardrails

- Do not simulate thousands of individual facilities.
- Do not create routine repair chores.
- Forecastable modifiers may change optimal planning; hidden offline disasters may not.
- New currencies require an accepted design decision.
- Requests range from approximately 45 minutes to 8 hours on first arrival.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Each era has a distinct planning question
- [ ] Multiple jobs do not make the phone UI unreadable
- [ ] Automation reduces chores without removing strategic choices
- [ ] Prestige builds show measurable expansion into later eras
- [ ] Save size and offline calculation remain within budgets
- [ ] Earlier infrastructure retains documented relevance

## Stop condition

Do not begin planetary content until the four-era balance arc is playtested.
