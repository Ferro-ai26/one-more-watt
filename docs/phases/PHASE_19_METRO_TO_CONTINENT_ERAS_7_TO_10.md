# Phase 19 — Metro to Continent: Eras 7–10

## Objective

Expand the proven idle loop through Metropolitan Grid, Regional Utility, National Compute Grid, and Continental Supergrid without multiplying chores or losing mobile readability.

## Preproduction gate

Before coding, create and approve one appendix per era defining requests, infrastructure, distinctive planning question, duration targets, WATT behavior/appearance, environment, asset list, automation, balance, and prestige interaction.

## Intended systems

- Multiple request slots or data-center campuses
- Regional interconnects and shared Reserve
- Higher-level automation policies
- Predictable weather, daylight, and time-zone modifiers
- Additional Model Weight branches
- Cross-era category synergies
- Requests ranging from roughly 45 minutes to 8 hours on first arrival

## Visual-production requirement

Each era is produced using the established theme system and includes complete environment, WATT evolution, infrastructure presentation, icons, effects, transitions, and audio. An era is not complete while using permanent gray-box assets.

## Guardrails

- Do not simulate thousands of individual facilities.
- Do not create routine repair chores.
- Hidden offline disasters are prohibited.
- New currencies require accepted decisions.
- Multiple jobs must remain readable on small screens.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Four era appendices approved
- [ ] Each era adds a distinct planning question
- [ ] Multi-job UI remains readable
- [ ] Automation reduces repetition without solving all strategy
- [ ] Earlier infrastructure retains relevance
- [ ] Each era has complete production presentation
- [ ] Save size/offline calculation/performance remain within budgets
- [ ] Android milestone playtests and balance evidence recorded

## Stop condition

Do not begin planetary production until the four-era arc is reviewed and approved.
