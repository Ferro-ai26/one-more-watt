# Phase 11 — Theme and Art Direction

## Objective

Explore, compare, and approve one coherent visual identity for ONE MORE WATT before production skinning.

## Player-facing result

No complete reskin is expected. The phase produces approved representative concepts showing what the finished game should become.

## Required reading

`GAME_VISION.md`, `WATT_CHARACTER_BIBLE.md`, `THEME_AND_ART_DIRECTION_SPEC.md`, `ART_DIRECTION_WORKBOOK.md`, `UI_UX_SPEC.md`, `ART_AND_AUDIO_DIRECTION.md`, and `ASSET_PIPELINE_AND_INVENTORY.md`.

## Included

- Audit current prototype visuals and reusable strengths
- Identify visual problems and production constraints
- Develop up to three distinct direction candidates
- Define candidate palette, typography, WATT treatment, UI language, environment language, motion, and cost/risk
- Produce representative Main Grid concepts for comparison
- Select one direction with explicit user approval
- Complete exact semantic design tokens
- Produce approved concepts for Main Grid, Build states, Performance Report, Brownout, and Era 1-to-2 transition
- Create WATT model sheet and Eras 1–3 evolution plan
- Record asset sourcing and licensing approach

## Excluded

- Applying the chosen style across the full game
- Rewriting gameplay
- Adding Era 4
- Mixing rejected candidates into the approved system
- Treating generated concept art as automatically production-ready

## Sol responsibilities

Sol may generate coded mockups, Godot prototype scenes, controlled asset concepts, palettes, component samples, shaders, or comparison boards. It must present tradeoffs and wait for explicit approval before locking the direction.

## Required artifacts

- Completed `ART_DIRECTION_WORKBOOK.md`
- Candidate comparison
- Approved direction decision
- Exact color/type/geometry/motion tokens
- WATT model sheet
- Five representative screen/state artifacts
- Initial asset inventory and production estimate
- Risks and mitigation list

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] No more than three coherent candidates were compared
- [ ] One candidate is explicitly approved by the user
- [ ] Representative screens agree visually
- [ ] WATT remains recognizable and central
- [ ] Phone-scale readability is demonstrated
- [ ] Tokens are exact enough for implementation
- [ ] Asset production and rights approach is credible
- [ ] Rejected elements are recorded
- [ ] No broad unapproved skinning occurred

## Stop condition

Stop after visual-direction approval. Do not apply the production skin across the prototype.
