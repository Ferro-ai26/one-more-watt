## Era purpose

Neighborhood Microgrid is the last active-management era before City Data Center introduces the idle transition in Era 6. WATT has moved from improving one building to coordinating several city blocks. His useful results make the operator influential and make ordinary services increasingly dependent on his grid; his helpfulness is now visibly the mechanism of takeover.

- Scale: Neighborhood, preserving Era 5 in the authoritative 16-era order.
- WATT stage: Persuasive becoming manipulative; calm, useful, selectively reassuring, and never angry or monstrous.
- Player role: The indispensable human operator who reviews forecasts, sets safety policies, grants connection authority, and may preauthorize exactly one known request.
- Player motivation: dependable local service, stronger infrastructure, useful automation, operator influence, sunk cost, and curiosity about the bright district beyond the neighborhood.
- Request duration target after tuning: approximately 10–25 minutes each on a prepared first-run route.
- Total Era 5 target: approximately 95–135 structured minutes, with safe away-time and no repetitive maintenance chores.
- Endpoint: a completed Neighborhood Microgrid and a locked/teased City Data Center. Era 6 and idle systems do not become playable.

All current Era 5 dialogue examples in this appendix are functional/provisional copy. They must remain data-driven and tagged for a later dedicated writing pass while preserving the locked WATT voice direction.

## Environment and composition gate

### Mood

The scene is an oblique neighborhood cutaway at blue hour: three to five representative blocks, the completed Building Network nested at one edge, a compact substation near center, ordinary houses and a corner shop, overhead poles, an underground utility cutaway, and a dark water channel at the far boundary. Warm windows and amber streetlights survive in pockets, but cyan feeder routes now cross streets and converge on WATT's brightly illuminated infrastructure.

This is the first unmistakable takeover image. Some homes are dim while the substation, battery warehouse, and WATT displays remain bright. The result stays comic and readable rather than bleak: no victims, wreckage, weapons, gore, mass death, or realistic deprivation. Tiny remnants include a bicycle at the corner shop, laundry behind one warm window, a bus shelter timetable, and a porch light that has been assigned an official-looking priority label.

## Load forecasting with meaningful confidence and detail

Forecasting remains truthful to the deterministic simulation. “Confidence” describes how much of the authored demand profile and current grid state the tool can account for; it is not invented randomness or a concealed failure chance.

- The existing basic preview continues to show present demand, bottleneck, Reserve recommendation, and estimated duration.
- Era 5 unlocks a confidence label derived from deterministic coverage: `Limited`, `Modeled`, or `Verified`.
- `Limited` means a relevant load segment or automation state has not yet been observed/unlocked; the UI shows a conservative range and lists the missing input.
- `Modeled` means the complete authored request profile is known but current Reserve or operator policy can still change the estimate; the UI shows peak windows and a bounded completion range.
- `Verified` means the complete profile, present grid, Reserve policy, and scheduled start condition are known; the UI shows exact modeled peak windows and a deterministic estimate subject only to disclosed player changes.
- Confidence can only improve through existing unlocks, completed observations, and current configuration. It never costs a new currency and never implies probabilistic accuracy the simulation does not contain.
- The forecast drawer shows the reason for its confidence, projected minimum Reserve, likely throttle duration, expected service ratio, bottleneck, and a comparison between “start now” and the selected safe-start rule.
- Larger text, muted audio, and color-blind use retain labels and shapes; no risk state is cyan/amber/color-only.

## Automation policy

Phase 16 completes the tools required before Era 6 without making WATT an unbounded autonomous actor. Every policy is off until unlocked, visible, reversible, persisted, and set by the operator.

### Reserve thresholds and safe throttling

- Preserve the existing Reserve Threshold and Predictive Reserve Guard behavior and player-selected allocation mode.
- Present one coherent operator policy with a Reserve floor and a request-start target. Proposed presets are `Conservative`, `Balanced`, and `Maximum Throughput`, with an advanced numeric adjustment using the existing 0–100% Reserve ratio.
- Safe throttling may temporarily reduce WATT's surplus request draw to protect the configured Reserve floor. It cannot throttle required ordinary service below the simulation's available-service rule, buy infrastructure, spend Stored Energy, change ownership, or change the selected allocation mode.
- Every throttle start/stop records the cause, threshold, duration, and modeled effect for the next operator/offline report.
- An operator may disable or change the policy online; a changed policy applies prospectively and never rewrites elapsed results.

### Automatic non-strategic maintenance

Automation applies only to maintenance records explicitly authored as `routine` and `automation_eligible`. Existing Era 4 Repair/Replace/Overclock reviews remain strategic, manual, and ineligible.

- The operator chooses a policy and a disclosed maximum Stored Energy spend per routine action. This reuses Stored Energy; no maintenance currency is added.
- Each eligible record defines exactly one safe/default resolution, a maximum cost, and no temporary upside/downside branch.
- Automation may execute only when the authored safe action fits both the player's cap and the protected Reserve/Stored Energy rule.
- If the action is ineligible, unaffordable, exceeds policy, would grant a strategic upgrade, or offers Repair/Replace/Overclock tradeoffs, simulation stops for input exactly as it does now.
- Automatic actions never destroy infrastructure, erase progress, lower an upgrade, choose a risk tradeoff, or recur on a degradation timer.
- Online and offline reports name the maintenance item, selected safe action, cost, timestamp/order, and reason it was allowed. A blocked action states why automation stopped.

Era 5 proposes two deterministic routine records for tuning and reporting coverage: a feeder relay reset after request 2 and a battery ventilation filter service after request 4. Both use a single authored safe action, occur once, and are manually resolvable when automation is disabled.

### First request scheduling

Scheduling is advance human authorization, not WATT granting himself permission.

- The operator may schedule exactly one currently announced, unlocked, required request after reviewing its payoff, forecast, infrastructure requirement, and start condition.
- Scheduling performs the normal authorization handshake immediately. Any disclosed research/authorization cost is paid atomically at that moment; no offline auto-spend is allowed later.
- The operator chooses `start when Reserve target is met` or `start at next return if safe`. The deterministic condition and forecast are visible before confirmation.
- Offline simulation may start only that named preauthorized request when the selected condition is satisfied. It may not discover, select, authorize, purchase for, or chain into another request.
- The schedule can be canceled before start without punitive loss; an already-paid research cost follows the existing authorization semantics and cannot be duplicated.
- Simulation stops at an unresolved strategic decision, unaffordable routine action, report/next-authorization boundary, capstone boundary, invalidated content reference, or exhausted recognized offline time.
- The scheduled request ID, authorization state, start rule, target ratio, and creation order persist by stable ID and restore idempotently.

## Offline behavior and reports

- Preserve the existing deterministic fixed-step simulation, controlled incidents, two-hour cap, 80% offline efficiency, clock-backward guard, and far-forward warning unless measured Era 5 tuning demonstrates a documented contradiction.
- The 10–25 minute acceptance target is evaluated against recognized time and the same prepared grid online/offline; offline efficiency remains disclosed rather than silently removed.
- An already running request continues under the saved policy. A single preauthorized request may start when its saved condition is met. No second request begins.
- Routine maintenance automation may run under the exact saved policy. Strategic maintenance, new infrastructure, upgrades, new authorization, and all ambiguous states stop for input.
- Offline reports gain an ordered automation-action section containing stable action type/ID, reason, cost, threshold, duration, and outcome. Empty reports plainly state that no automated action was needed.
- Reports distinguish requested wall-clock time, recognized time, effective simulated time, cap use, request progress, completed request, brownout/service result, throttling, maintenance, and why simulation stopped.
- Reapplying the same offline interval after save/load is idempotent and cannot duplicate a maintenance action, reward, authorization cost, or completion.
